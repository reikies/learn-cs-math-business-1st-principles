# The Complete Map of Business & Entrepreneurship

> A business is a machine that creates value for others and captures a fraction of
> that value for itself. But understanding the machine requires three lenses:
> **Product Thinking** (should this exist?), **Marketing & Distribution** (how do
> people find it?), and **Business & Entrepreneurship** (how does it make money and
> survive?). These three disciplines are the trinity of building anything that lasts.
> Master one and you're dangerous. Master all three and you're unstoppable.

---

## The Three Pillars

### Pillar 1: Product Thinking — Should this exist?

This is different from engineering. Engineering asks "how do I build this?" Product thinking asks "should this exist?" Most products fail not because the technology is bad, but because the product is bad — it doesn't solve a real problem, or the UX makes the value invisible.

| Topic | What it is | The question it answers |
|-------|-----------|------------------------|
| [Product Thinking](03-customers/product-thinking.md) | User research, prototyping, iteration, product taste, the AI product gap | How do I know I'm building the right thing — and when do I ship ugly vs. polish? |
| [Product](03-customers/product.md) | Customer discovery, PMF, MVPs, prioritization, metrics, PLG mechanics | What's the process for finding product-market fit? |

### Pillar 2: Marketing & Distribution — How do people find it?

The hardest part of making money isn't building — it's getting people to know your thing exists. This is especially true for AI and technology products, where you need to educate the market before you can sell to it.

| Topic | What it is | The question it answers |
|-------|-----------|------------------------|
| [Marketing & Distribution](03-customers/marketing-and-distribution.md) | Content marketing, SEO, community building, PLG vs. SLG, channel strategy, AI distribution | How do I build distribution that compounds — not just buy attention that disappears? |
| [Marketing](03-customers/marketing.md) | Positioning, brand, funnel mechanics, acquisition channels, B2B vs. B2C | How do I position my product and reach the right audience? |

### Pillar 3: Business & Entrepreneurship — How does this make money and survive?

Understanding the economics, strategy, finance, and organizational machinery that turns a product into a sustainable business.

| Topic | What it is | The question it answers |
|-------|-----------|------------------------|
| [Business Models](02-strategy/business-models.md) | Revenue patterns (SaaS, marketplace, freemium, usage-based), unit economics, pricing | How do I charge for this — especially AI products? |
| [Sales](03-customers/sales.md) | B2B sales, enterprise deals, pricing negotiation, sales models, comp structures | How do I convert interest into revenue — especially in B2B where margins are highest? |
| [Fundraising & VC](05-finance/fundraising.md) | VC mechanics, funding stages, SAFEs, term sheets, dilution, bootstrapping | Do I raise money or bootstrap? If I raise, how does it work? |
| [The Startup Journey](07-entrepreneurship/the-startup-journey.md) | Stages, pivots, team building, psychology, common mistakes | What does the full arc of building a company look like? |

## The Supporting Layers

The three pillars don't float in the air. They rest on a foundation of economics, law, strategy, operations, people, and finance. Each layer builds on the ones below it.

---

### Layer 1: Foundations — The ground rules of commerce

Before you can start a business, you need to understand the world it operates in. How do markets work? How do you keep score? What are the rules?

| Topic | What it is | Why it exists |
|-------|-----------|---------------|
| [Economics](01-foundations/economics.md) | Supply, demand, markets, incentives, competition, trade-offs | Every business decision is an economic decision. You can't navigate a market you don't understand. |
| [Accounting & Financial Literacy](01-foundations/accounting.md) | Balance sheets, income statements, cash flow, double-entry bookkeeping | Accounting is the language of business. If you can't read financial statements, you're flying blind. |
| [Legal Foundations](01-foundations/legal-foundations.md) | Business entities, contracts, intellectual property, liability, regulation | The legal system defines what you can and can't do. Ignorance isn't a defense — it's a bankruptcy filing. |

---

### Layer 2: Strategy — Why some businesses win

Most businesses fail. The ones that survive understand the competitive landscape and position themselves where they can win.

| Topic | What it is | Why it exists |
|-------|-----------|---------------|
| [Competitive Strategy](02-strategy/competitive-strategy.md) | Porter's forces, positioning, moats, disruption, first-mover vs. fast-follower | Doing something isn't enough. You need to do something that others can't easily replicate. |
| [Business Models](02-strategy/business-models.md) | How businesses create, deliver, and capture value — SaaS, marketplaces, freemium, licensing | A great product with no business model is a hobby. The model determines whether you survive. |

---

### Layer 3: Customers — Finding, serving, and reaching demand

Strategy tells you where to play. This layer is about who you're building for, what you're building, how you reach them, and how you sell to them.

| Topic | What it is | Why it exists |
|-------|-----------|---------------|
| [Product Thinking](03-customers/product-thinking.md) | The "should this exist?" discipline — user research, prototyping, shipping judgment, AI product design | Building the wrong thing is the most expensive mistake. Product thinking prevents it. |
| [Product](03-customers/product.md) | Customer discovery, product-market fit, MVPs, prioritization, metrics, PLG | Building what people actually want is the hardest problem in business. This is the process. |
| [Marketing & Distribution](03-customers/marketing-and-distribution.md) | Content marketing, SEO, community building, PLG vs. SLG, channel strategy | The best product in the world fails if nobody knows it exists. Distribution is the main quest. |
| [Marketing](03-customers/marketing.md) | Positioning, brand, funnel mechanics, acquisition channels, B2B vs. B2C | How demand finds supply — the mechanics of reaching and converting an audience. |
| [Sales](03-customers/sales.md) | Pipelines, pricing, negotiation, enterprise vs. self-serve, compensation | Marketing creates interest. Sales converts interest into revenue. This is where money changes hands. |

---

### Layer 4: Operations & People — The organizational machine

Every business runs on cash. Finance is how you fund growth, measure performance, and make decisions with numbers.

| [Fundraising & Venture Capital](05-finance/fundraising.md) | Equity, dilution, term sheets, bootstrapping vs. raising, investor relations | Building something big often requires more capital than you have. Fundraising is how you trade equity for acceleration. |
| [Financial Modeling & Metrics](05-finance/financial-modeling.md) | Unit economics, forecasting, KPIs, dashboards, scenario analysis | You can't improve what you don't measure. Models turn intuition into testable predictions. |

### Force Multipliers — Skills that compound everything

These aren't fields — they're skills. They don't sit in one layer; they cut across all of them. Writing makes your marketing, sales, fundraising, and product better. Data literacy makes every decision sharper. Design taste makes every surface more effective. Psychology is the operating system behind all human interaction. Invest in any of these and the returns compound across everything else you do.

| Topic | What it is | Why it exists |
|-------|-----------|---------------|
| [Writing & Communication](08-force-multipliers/writing-and-communication.md) | Clear writing, persuasive writing, technical writing for non-technical audiences, product copy, pitches, prompts | Every business skill is delivered through writing. If you can't write clearly, you can't sell, market, lead, or fundraise. |
| [Data Literacy & Analytics](08-force-multipliers/data-literacy.md) | Reading charts, understanding distributions, business metrics (CAC, LTV, churn, NRR), cohort analysis, SQL, data storytelling | Every company drowns in data. The people who make data useful get listened to. "I can make your data useful with AI" is a killer pitch. |
| [Design (Basic UI/UX)](08-force-multipliers/design-basics.md) | Information hierarchy, spacing, typography, color, user flows, AI product design patterns | You don't need to be a designer. You need enough taste to not build ugly, confusing things. AI products that look good get adopted faster. |
| [Psychology & Behavioral Science](08-force-multipliers/psychology-and-behavior.md) | Cognitive biases, decision-making, motivation, habit formation, trust, persuasion, pricing psychology | Marketing, UX, sales, negotiation, and prompt engineering are all applied psychology. Understand why people do what they do and everything else gets easier. |

## What Powers What

| Business | Layers it runs on |
|---|---|
| Launching a SaaS startup | Product thinking (problem worth solving?) → product (MVP, PMF) → distribution (content, SEO, community) → sales (self-serve + enterprise) → fundraising → growth |
| Opening a coffee shop | Economics (local demand) → legal (LLC, permits) → accounting (P&L) → operations (supply chain) → people (hiring baristas) |
| Building an AI product | Product thinking (does the AI solve a real workflow problem?) → product (prototype, iterate) → distribution (educate market, show don't tell) → business model (per-seat, usage-based, outcome-based) → growth |
| Acquiring a competitor | Strategy (market consolidation) → corporate finance (valuation, due diligence) → legal (contracts, antitrust) → operations (integration) → people (reorgs) |
| Pivoting a failing startup | Product thinking (is the problem real?) → product (customer discovery) → strategy (new positioning) → financial modeling (runway analysis) → distribution (relaunch) |
| Taking a company public (IPO) | Accounting (audited financials) → corporate finance (valuation, underwriting) → legal (SEC compliance) → growth (proving the narrative) |
| Expanding internationally | Legal (local regulations) → operations (logistics, localization) → distribution (cultural adaptation, local channels) → finance (currency, tax) → people (local hiring) |
| Franchising a restaurant chain | Business models (franchise model) → legal (franchise agreements) → operations (standardized playbook) → finance (unit economics) → growth (scaling) |

## Recommended Study Order

**Phase 1 — Foundations:** Economics → Accounting → Legal Foundations

**Phase 2 — Strategy:** Competitive Strategy → Business Models

**Phase 3 — The Trinity:**
- Product Thinking → Product (the "should this exist?" lens, then the PMF/MVP mechanics)
- Marketing & Distribution → Marketing (the distribution strategy, then funnel mechanics)
- Sales (converting demand into revenue)

**Phase 4 — Operations:** Operations → People & Organizations

**Phase 5 — Finance:** Corporate Finance → Fundraising → Financial Modeling

**Phase 6 — Growth:** Growth & Scaling

**Phase 7 — Entrepreneurship:** The Startup Journey

**Anytime — Force Multipliers:** Writing & Communication, Data Literacy, Design Basics, Psychology & Behavioral Science
*(These aren't sequential — pick them up whenever, and they make everything else better.)*
